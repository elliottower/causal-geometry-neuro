## The Core Problem You're Correctly Worried About

**Your H¹ >> 0 result is almost certainly an artifact, and here's exactly why.**

### Why the Restriction Maps are Garbage

The sheaf setup requires that for each edge (region A → region B), you have a restriction map ρ_{AB}: Gr(k_A, N_A) → Gr(k_B, N_B) that sends "the choice subspace at A" to "what it should look like at B." In your implementation in exp7_sheaf_cohomology.py, this is estimated as a normalized cross-correlation matrix: W = X_B^T X_A / n from ~250 trials.

The problem is dimensional. MOs lives in R^30, ACA in R^25 — these are different ambient spaces. The "restriction map" between them is a 25 × 30 matrix estimated from 250 data points. With 750 free parameters and 250 samples, you're in a severely underdetermined regime — the matrix is almost entirely noise. Sheaf cohomology was designed for cases where restriction maps are exact (algebraic topology) or at least consistent (cellular sheaves on graphs). When the restriction maps are noise, H¹ >> 0 is the trivially expected outcome for any set of subspaces, meaningful or not.

In transformers, this problem disappears entirely because all layers share the same d_model = 768-dimensional residual stream, so restriction maps are square identity matrices and the comparison is well-posed. That's why your transformer OASR result (H¹ ≈ 0) is interpretable but your neural result probably isn't.

### The Two Failure Modes in the Code

There are actually two distinct problems stacked on top of each other:

1. Subspace estimation uses PCA, not DAS. fit_pca_subspace recovers the top-variance subspace, not the causally active subspace. The choice direction might explain 3% of variance in a region with a huge locomotion signal dominating the first PCs. PCA will give you the wrong subspace to put in the sheaf stalk.

2. Cross-region connectivity is trial-average correlation, not effective connectivity. The "restriction map" in _build_sheaf is literally (mean2.T @ mean1) / n_trials — mean firing rates correlated across regions, normalized. This is a measure of co-firing, not of how information from one region's causal subspace arrives at another region's neurons. It's not what sheaf theory needs.

The result H¹ ∈ {75, 495} is in the range expected from the random subspace control — which is why that control is the key test.
