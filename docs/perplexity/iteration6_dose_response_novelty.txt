## Perplexity Deep Dive: Dose-Response Novelty Claim (June 2026)

### Search 1: Is graded dose-response on subspace dimensions novel?

**Answer: "Among the first" — not "the first"**

**Closest paper:** Neural dynamics outside task-coding dimensions drive decision trajectories
(Pereira-Obilinovic, Daie, Chen, Svoboda, Darshan, bioRxiv Nov 2025)
https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.11.20.689599v1

- Records from ALM + motor thalamus in mice, delayed-response task
- Perturbs individual subspace dimensions (choice axis vs residual)
- Causal measure: model-based perturbation of fitted RNN, validated with two-photon optogenetics
- Finding: residual dimensions flip choices, choice axis does NOT
- This is dimension-level causal perturbation — but categorical (coding vs residual), not graded

**Multiplexed Subspaces** (Princeton, through 2025): relates neural responses to individual
subspace dimensions, but causal measure is functional connectivity, not formal intervention.

**Our specific combination is defensibly novel:**
1. Dose-response framing — progressive, graded dimension removal (1, then 2, then 3...)
   producing a monotonic curve. The ALM paper does categorical contrast, not graded sweep.
2. IIA/DAS (interchange interventions from Geiger et al.) rather than model-based or
   optogenetic perturbation. IIA lives in ML interpretability, not neuro.

### Search 2: Deeper dive on the actual papers

**Pereira-Obilinovic et al. 2025 (Svoboda/Darshan):**
- Heavyweight paper (Svoboda is major figure)
- Causal claim measured IN the RNN model, validated against two-photon optogenetics
- They ask "which subspace is causal?" — we ask "how does causal effect SCALE as you
  progressively remove dimensions?"
- ANY reviewer in this subfield will know this paper — MUST cite and position against it

**ML-side threat — A Low-Rank Subspace Analysis of LLM Interventions (2026):**
https://arxiv.org/html/2606.14388v1
- Literally varies category subspace rank r ∈ {1,2,3} and measures effects
- That IS a dose-response sweep over subspace dimensions with causal intervention
- Exactly our method, just in LLMs not brains

**A Geometric Notion of Causal Probing:** interventions splitting concept subspace from
orthogonal complement. IIA/interchange-intervention-on-subspaces well established in DAS/pyvene.

### What we CAN and CANNOT say:

CANNOT: "first to apply dose-response to subspace dimensions" (falsified by LLM rank paper)
CANNOT: "first to causally perturb individual subspace dimensions in neural data" (falsified by Pereira-Obilinovic)

CAN say: "Causal perturbation of neural subspace dimensions has recently been demonstrated
in motor cortex, contrasting coding versus residual subspaces [Pereira-Obilinovic et al. 2025].
Graded, dimension-by-dimension dose-response analysis using interchange interventions (DAS/IIA)
— rather than model-based or optogenetic perturbation — has been explored in language-model
interpretability but not, to our knowledge, applied to biological population recordings.
We bring these two threads together."

### Strategic warning:
Svoboda/Darshan finding (choice axis is causally INERT, residual dimensions drive behavior)
could be a confound for our interpretation. Our ~10% IIA flip rate might be modest BECAUSE
we're intervening on the coding subspace. Should run progressive ablation on BOTH coding
and residual subspaces and compare — either replicates their finding (citation magnet) or
shows cross-dataset disagreement (also interesting).
